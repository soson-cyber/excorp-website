# Research-Backed Compose

Use this reference when `Compose` needs facts from public sources rather than a user-supplied evidence packet.

## Evidence Acquisition

1. Define the research question, audience, geography or system boundary, and evidence cutoff date.
2. Use an available research or browsing capability. For current, niche, consequential, or version-sensitive claims,
   do not rely on model memory.
3. Prefer primary sources: official documentation, standards, original research, government data, vendor release
   documentation, and first-party survey reports. Use secondary analysis only to add context or identify disagreement.
4. For trend claims, seek multiple independent sources and distinguish measured adoption, announced intent, vendor
   marketing, and the writer's inference. Do not turn one survey into a universal market conclusion.
5. For technical comparisons, use official documentation and primary architecture material. Compare like with like;
   distinguish a library or framework from the architecture style it supports.
6. Record a source ledger with claim, source, publication or update date, scope, and status: observed fact, source
   claim, inference, disagreement, or unknown.

Search snippets are discovery aids, not evidence. Open the supporting source before using the claim. A publicly
available source can still be stale, promotional, geographically narrow, methodologically weak, or contradicted.

## Compose From The Ledger

- State the evidence cutoff date near claims described as current.
- Cite material external claims with direct links. Put citations next to the claim they support.
- Synthesize across sources; do not concatenate summaries source by source.
- Separate shared findings, meaningful disagreements, bounded inference, and open questions.
- Preserve source scope: geography, industry, sample, version, measurement method, and confidence.
- Prefer decision criteria and tradeoffs over a universal winner or a list of fashionable terms.
- Use the target audience's language directly. Research in English does not require an English draft before Korean
  composition.

## Stop Conditions

Return a partial document and `Needs Human` when:

- research access is unavailable,
- material claims cannot be traced to opened sources,
- sources conflict and the requested conclusion depends on choosing one,
- current evidence is too narrow for the requested geography, industry, or product version,
- a paywalled or inaccessible source is the only support for a material claim.

Do not hide these limits in a generic disclaimer. Name the affected claim and the evidence needed to resolve it.
